About

个人简介 / Personal Profile

I am a university teacher working in bioinformatics and genomics. My academic interests center on genome-scale variation, population history, introgression, mutation load, and computational approaches for interpreting biological data.

My teaching focuses on helping students build practical programming and research-computing skills through Java, R, Linux, and bioinformatics coursework. I value clear reasoning, reproducible workflows, and hands-on practice with real biological datasets.

Research

Research Interests

Research areas include crop genomics, population-level genetic variation, and computational biology.

Wheat Genomics

Genome variation, domestication, adaptation, and trait-related genomic regions in wheat.

Population Genetics

Genetic diversity, structure, demographic history, and selection across populations.

Bioinformatics

Computational pipelines, reproducible analysis, and biological interpretation of large datasets.

Introgression

Detection and interpretation of gene flow, hybridization, and adaptive introgression.

Mutation Load

Assessment of deleterious variation and its evolutionary and breeding implications.
